1. Paracetamol
Use: Pain relief, fever reduction. Used for headaches, muscle aches, arthritis, backaches, toothaches, colds, and fevers.

Chemical Composition: Paracetamol (also known as acetaminophen)

Dosage Information:

Forms: Tablets, capsules, liquid suspension, effervescent tablets
Strengths: 500 mg, 650 mg, 1000 mg
Side Effects:

Common: Nausea, rash
Severe: Liver damage (in overdose), allergic reactions
Brand Names: Crocin, Calpol, Tylenol

Usage Instructions:

Take with or without food.
Avoid alcohol as it can increase the risk of liver damage.
Do not exceed 4 grams per day to avoid liver toxicity.



2. Aspirin
Use: Pain relief, anti-inflammatory, fever reduction, blood thinner. Used for headaches, muscle pain, arthritis, and to reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Chemical Composition: Acetylsalicylic acid

Dosage Information:

Forms: Tablets, chewable tablets, enteric-coated tablets
Strengths: 75 mg, 150 mg, 300 mg, 500 mg
Side Effects:

Common: Upset stomach, heartburn
Severe: Bleeding, ulcers, allergic reactions, Reye's syndrome in children
Brand Names: Aspirin, Ecosprin, Disprin

Usage Instructions:

Take with food or a full glass of water to minimize stomach upset.
Do not use in children with viral infections due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
Avoid alcohol and other NSAIDs to reduce the risk of bleeding.
